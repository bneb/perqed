## Problem: Schur Number Lower Bound S(6) ≥ 537

### Statement
The Schur number S(r) is the largest N such that {1, ..., N} can be r-colored with no color class containing a solution to x + y = z. We want to prove S(6) ≥ 537 by exhibiting a valid 6-coloring.

**Important:** S(6) is NOT known exactly. S(5) = 160 is the only solved case. S(6) ≥ 536 is a known lower bound (Fredricksen & Sweet). The exact value of S(6) is completely open. Finding a valid 6-coloring of {1..537} is a plausible open problem — do NOT assume it is impossible or already disproved.

### Lean 4 Theorem Signature (use EXACTLY this — do NOT invent helper predicates)
```
: ∃ (f : Fin 537 → Fin 6),
    ∀ (x y z : Fin 537),
      (x.val + 1) + (y.val + 1) = (z.val + 1) →
      ¬ (f x = f y ∧ f y = f z)
```

### Search Strategy
- problem_class: "schur_partition", domain_size: 537, num_partitions: 6
- The SA engine will search for a partition_rule_js (body of `function(i)` where i is 1-indexed, returns 0–5) that achieves E=0 (zero monochromatic x+y=z triples)
- Energy function: for each color class P_c, count pairs (x,y) in P_c where x+y is also in P_c
- CRITICAL: partition_rule_js MUST use an explicit `return` statement. A bare expression (no return) scores E=domain_size.

### Lean Proof Strategy (after SA finds E=0 witness)
The proof is purely constructive:
1. `exact ⟨myColoring, by decide⟩` where `myColoring` is a `Fin 537 → Fin 6` defined as a lookup table from the SA witness
2. `decide` is feasible because the predicate is a finite check over Fin 537 × Fin 537 × Fin 537
